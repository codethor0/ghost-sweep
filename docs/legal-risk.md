# Legal Risk Guidance

ghost-sweep provides informational risk signals based on user reports and employer responses. It does not provide legal advice.

## What the platform is

- A structured reporting and transparency tool
- A source of user-reported hiring integrity signals
- A system that encourages evidence and employer response

## What the platform is not

- A court, regulator, or law enforcement body
- A definitive fraud determination service
- A substitute for direct communication with employers or legal counsel

## Language standards

Public copy and score explanations must avoid unsupported accusations. Preferred terms include risk signal, reported by users, unverified, verified active, disputed, and evidence reviewed.

## Evidence expectations

Reports should rely on verifiable hiring artifacts such as posting URLs, dated screenshots, repost history, and public employer statements. Moderators may remove reports that lack adequate support.

## Employer dispute path

Employers need a fair process to respond, verify active roles, and dispute incorrect or stale information. Disputed and verified states affect scoring adjustments.

## Defamation risk mitigation

- Require evidence with every report
- Document scoring inputs and weights
- Provide employer response and dispute mechanisms
- Avoid categorical fraud language
- Support moderation review before high-visibility promotion of reports

## Privacy and retaliation risk

Do not encourage users to publish personal contact details of hiring managers or internal employees. Minimize storage of sensitive personal data and provide removal workflows for accidental submissions.

## Jurisdiction note

Legal obligations vary by region. Production deployment requires independent legal review before marketing the service broadly.

## Maintainer action

If you receive a legal complaint or takedown request, route it through private maintainer review and document the decision without exposing private user data publicly.
